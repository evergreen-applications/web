Privacy Policy

Last updated: [July 18, 2026]

1. What we collect

TrackPack is built to collect as little as possible:

  • Apple ID identifier. When you sign in with Apple, we receive a stable identifier for your account, and — only on your very first sign-in ever — the name you choose to share.
  • Tracking numbers and carrier codes. When you add or refresh a package, the tracking number and (if known) carrier are sent to our backend so it can retrieve status from the relevant carrier or tracking service.
  • Package data you enter. Labels, directions (incoming/outgoing), and return deadlines you add for your own packages.

We don't collect your name, email address, physical address, or payment details — subscriptions are handled entirely by Apple, and we never see your payment information.

2. Where your data lives

Your package list is stored in your own private iCloud account via Apple's CloudKit, not on servers we operate. We don't maintain a database of your packages. Our backend server only ever sees a tracking number and carrier code at the moment you look one up — it doesn't store your package list.

3. Who we share data with

To retrieve tracking status, the tracking number and carrier code for a package you're tracking are sent to:

  • Ship24, a tracking aggregator, for carriers without a direct integration.
  • UPS and FedEx directly, for packages identified as shipped by those carriers.

These providers receive only the tracking number and carrier code — never your Apple ID, name, or any other package's data. We also rely on Apple for Sign in with Apple, iCloud sync, push notifications, and subscription processing, each governed by Apple's own privacy practices.

4. What we don't do

  • We don't sell your data.
  • We don't use advertising or third-party analytics SDKs.
  • We don't track you across other apps or websites.

5. Camera access

If you use the camera to scan a tracking number off a label, the image is processed on your device to recognize text. Nothing from the camera is uploaded or stored.

6. Data retention and deletion

Because your package data lives in your own iCloud account, deleting the TrackPack app or signing out doesn't delete it — it remains in your iCloud until you remove it yourself (by deleting packages in the app, which removes them from iCloud too). To fully remove TrackPack's data, delete all tracked packages before deleting the app, or manage TrackPack's iCloud data from your device's Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud.

7. Children's privacy

TrackPack is not directed at children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect data from them.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as TrackPack changes. Material changes will be reflected by an updated "Last updated" date above.

9. Contact

Questions about this policy or your data can be sent to trackpack-support@evergreen-applications.com.
